Urn Draws

In this experiment we will secretly draw two balls for you and then put them back in the urn. The first ball is drawn from Urn 1, which contains ... blue and ... red balls. This means that the probability of drawing a blue ball is ...%. The second ball is drawn from Urn 2, which contains ... white and ... black balls. This means that the probability of drawing a white ball is ...%.

You will interact with another participant in this experiment. We will refer to this person as your partner. We will also secretly draw two balls for your partner using the same procedure. We will not tell you which ball we drew for you or your partner until the end of the experiment.

Decision 1

You will have to choose between three doors. There is a prize of ... behind some of the doors. If you pick a winning door, you will receive the prize. The number of winning doors depends on the ball, which we drew for you from Urn 1.

  • If the ball drawn from Urn 1 is blue, ... of the doors are winning doors.
  • If the ball drawn from Urn 1 is red, ... of the doors are winning doors.
We will not tell you whether you or your partner chose a winning door until the end of the experiment.

Information and Guesses

Afterwards, you will receive some information. We will show you a card that can be either blue or red. If we drew a blue ball for you, we will draw a card from a pile that contains ... blue and ... red cards. If we drew a red ball for you, we will draw a card from a pile that contains ... blue and ... red cards. The probability that your card's color matches your ball's color is ...%. Your partner will also see a card that depends on their ball's color. You will never learn about the color of your partner's card and your partner will never learn the color of your card.

Furthermore, we will tell you if you receive an orange star or not. You will only receive an orange star if we drew a white ball for you from Urn 2 and if you chose a winning door in the previous choice. If either the ball is black or you did not choose a winning door (or both), you will not receive an orange star. Your partner may also receive an orange star according to the same rules. We will let you know whether your partner received an orange star, and your partner will know whether you received one.

We will summarize all the information that you have received in a table like the one below. If you have not received information, the elements will be colored grey and have a question mark. After you have received information, the elements will be updated with the corresponding color.

After you have received the information we will ask you to evaluate how likely some statements are. For each statement there is a prize of .... The probability of winning the prize depends on your guess and whether the statement is true. To maximize the probability of earning the prize you should simply guess how likely each statement is true on average. We will show you the exact probabilities when you make the choice.

Decision 2

We will shuffle the prizes behind the doors so the winning doors may be in a different place than the first time. Both you and your partner again have ... doors to choose from. The number of winning doors again depends on the color of the ball that was drawn for you and your partner from Urn 1.

  • If the ball drawn from Urn 1 is blue, ... of the doors are winning doors.
  • If the ball drawn from Urn 1 is red, ... of the doors are winning doors.

  • Your partner will decide whether to choose the same door as you or to choose one of their doors.
  • If you choose a winning door, you will receive ...
  • Your partner's earnings then depend on their choice:
    • If your partner decides to follow your door choice and you choose a winning door, they will also receive ....
    • If your partner decides not to follow your door choice, they will choose from their own doors. Your partner will then receive ... if they choose a winning door.

If you decide not to choose from your own doors:

  • Your partner will choose a door from their doors.
  • If your partner chooses a winning door, both you and your partner will each receive ....

Survey

After the experiment there will be a short survey, for which you will earn some extra cash.

Payments

You will be paid either for the door choices in Decision 1 and Decision 2, or for your guesses, but not for both. Both are equally likely. At the end of the experiment, we will inform you whether you are paid for the doors or for your guesses. After the experiment, we will transfer your earning via Paypal to the email address that you specified in the beginning of the experiment.
